Which life sciences documents are in scope?

Select all that apply — each has different structure and extraction requirements

A

Case Report Forms (CRFs)

Clinical trial data collection forms — patient demographics, adverse events, visit data. Highly structured, strict schema.

B

Lab Notebooks / Research Notes

Handwritten experimental observations, protocols, results. Semi-structured to free-form. Common in R&D.

C

Regulatory Submissions / SOPs

FDA/EMA filings, standard operating procedures, batch records. Typed/native PDFs, structured sections.

D

Patient Records / Clinical Notes

Medical history, physician notes, discharge summaries. Mix of handwritten and typed, free-form narrative.

E

Lab Reports / Assay Results

Instrument-generated or manually filled reports — test results, compound data, QC records.

F

Manufacturing / Batch Records

GMP batch records, deviation reports, equipment logs. Printed forms with handwritten entries.
